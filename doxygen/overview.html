Overview

ESTER uses a Newton's method to solve a set of PDEs. Solving the PDE \( L(u) = 0 \tag{1} \) with Newton's method is done by refining the solution by solving: \( J_L(\delta u) = -L(u) \tag{2} \) And updating the solution with \( u = u + \delta u \)

In order to write an equation in ESTER formalism, one has to write it in the form of \( (2) \).

For instance the Poisson equation ( \( \Delta \phi = \pi_c \rho \), with \( \pi_c = \frac{4 \pi G \rho_c^2 R^2}{p_c} \)), is written in ESTER with: \( \Delta \phi - \pi_c \rho = 0 \) (to conform notation \( (1) \)).

This is equivalent to the code:

1 lap_phi=lap(phi);
2 lap_phi.add(op, "Phi", "Phi");
3 lap_phi.add(op, "Phi", "r");
4 
5 op->add_d("Phi", "rho", -\pi_c*ones(nr, nth));
6 op->add_d("Phi", "\pi_c", -rho);
7 
8 rhs1 = -lap_phi.eval()+\pi_c*rho;
9 rhs2=-lap_phi.eval();
10 
11 rhs=zeros(nr+nex,nth);
12 rhs.setblock(0,nr-1,0,-1,rhs1);
13 rhs.setblock(nr,nr+nex-1,0,-1,rhs2);
14 
15 op->set_rhs("Phi",rhs);

Line 1 defines the symbolic expression for \( \Delta \phi \) (see symbolic).

Lines 2 and 3 add the terms of \( \Delta \phi \) depending on \( \phi \) and \( r \) into the Jacobian matrix of the expression.

Lines 5 and 6 add the terms \( \frac{\partial}{\partial \rho} \pi_c \rho \) and \( \frac{\partial}{\partial \pi_c} \pi_c \rho \) into the Jacobian matrix, this completes the definition of the left hand side of the equation.

Line 8, and 9 build the right hand side for inner domains ( \( -\Delta \phi + \pi_c \rho \)), and the external domain ( \( -\Delta \phi \)).

Lines 11 to 13 combine the right hand side into a single matrix, and line 15 sets the right hand side of the equation in the solver.

Code Overview

The Newton iteration is performed in star2d::solve. The set of equation and boundary conditions are written in the solver in functions:

  • star2d::solve_definitions
  • star2d::solve_poisson
  • star2d::solve_mov
  • star2d::solve_temp
  • star2d::solve_dim
  • star2d::solve_map
  • star2d::solve_Omega
  • star2d::solve_atm
  • star2d::solve_gsup
  • star2d::solve_Teff

Function solver::solve stores all the terms registered in the operator in the previous steps into the operator matrix (see solver::wrap, solver::unwrap and solver::create). And then solves the matrix and update the solution the solution fields.
